TACTILE ACCESSIBLE COMPUTATIONAL THINKING (TACT)
GRAND CHALLENGE


Call for Accessible Games


Background

The pedagogy of Computational Thinking has been more visual than tactile, thereby leading to lack of inclusion in the classroom. The aspiration of this challenge is to create a novel approach to CT which can be equally accessible for learners with speech, hearing or visual impairment or those without any impairments.


The Grand Challenge

TACT GRAND CHALLENGE 2022 which has been a novel attempt at creating awareness about the need for inclusion in the CT academic and practitioners community. Entries were invited for the design of accessible games or challenging activities using tactile materials, i.e. activities which use touch as the principal medium. The games were expected to meet the learning objectives of Computational Thinking at an Elementary level (Grades 1 - 8) as per the CSpathshala Curriculum (https://cspathshala.org/curriculum/). 30 entries from various Indian states and some other countries. 12 Reviewers who double blind reviewed the games. Of the top 12 three games were presented online while others were presented through in person demos. The organising team had voting for the selection of the winners. 176 responses were recorded. The winners have been selected with equal weightage given to these votes and the scores given by the judges. The awards for the TACT Grand Challenge are sponsored by Microsoft Research India and each of the winners received a cash award.

Who may apply

Computer Scientists Staff and students of Mathematics and Computer Science at colleges and high schools. Designers and educators interested in computational thinking. All entries may be sent to ctis2022@cspathshala.org (in DOC or PDF format) by 30th May 2022 with the subject “Your name-Game Name-TACT Grand Challenge - CTIS 2022”. E.g. Ramanujam-BeadStringing-TACT Grand Challenge-CTIS2022. Please refer to the grand challenge template and samples provided for preparing your abstract. It is recommended to follow the format suggested by these samples.
Please send the abstract to ctis2022@cspathshala.org. The abstract may be in English, Hindi or Marathi. The names of all authors, the details of school affiliation and contact details are ESSENTIAL.

Submissions are closed.



Incentives

The top 20 entries will be selected by a team of reviewers composed of experts in CT and inclusive education. They will be provided with an opportunity to conduct a participatory workshop at the conference. These games will be published on the conference website. A brief video of the game would be very useful.

Objective of Conference session

This conference session is being planned as an exhibition / game pavilion where delegates spend time with each game design booth and play/interact with each creator. This session is intended to showcase the 20 best tactile Computational Thinking games designed by the CT community at large and experienced with the participating teachers at CTiS 2022. The top five of these games as voted by the delegates who engage with the designers will be awarded prizes by reputed sponsors. Their games will be included in the CSpathshala curriculum for wide circulation at schools.
